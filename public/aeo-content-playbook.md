# **AEO Content Structure Guide**

## **Meta Data Structure (Above the Fold)**

### **Meta Title**

* **Character count:** 50-53 characters (strict limit)  
* **Keyword placement:** Primary keyword FIRST  
* **Format:** `[Primary Keyword]: [Value Proposition] ([Year])`  
* **CTR optimization:** Use power words, numbers, or emotional triggers  
* **Example:** `Answer Engine Optimization: Get Cited in 30 Days (2025)`

### **Meta Description**

* **Character count:** 140 characters (strict limit)  
* **Keyword placement:** Primary keyword in first 5 words  
* **Format:** `[Primary Keyword] + [benefit] + [proof point] + [CTA]`  
* **CTR optimization:** Include numbers, data, or specific outcomes  
* **Example:** `Answer Engine Optimization gets you cited by ChatGPT. Real case: 93 citations in 90 days. Proven framework with Ahrefs data.`

### **URL Structure**

* **Format:** `domain.com/primary-keyword-topic`  
* **Length:** Under 60 characters  
* **Separators:** Use hyphens, not underscores  
* **Keywords:** Include primary keyword  
* **Avoid:** Stop words (the, a, and), dates, unnecessary words  
* **Example:** `seoengine.ai/answer-engine-optimization-guide`

## **Page Header Structure**

### **TL;DR Box (Position: First Element)**

* **Placement:** Immediately below H1, before any other content  
* **Length:** 2-3 sentences (40-60 words)  
* **Purpose:** Give AI systems and users instant answer  
* **Format:** Bold or boxed to stand out visually  
* **Content:** Summarize key takeaway, main data point, and primary benefit  
* **Example:**

```
Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) gets your brand cited by ChatGPT (58M monthly searches), Perplexity (377K searches), and Google AI Overviews (25K searches). We grew Qcall.ai from 0 to 93 ChatGPT citations in 90 days. This playbook shows the exact framework with proven tactics.
```

### **H1 (Main Headline)**

* **Count:** Exactly ONE H1 per page  
* **Length:** 60-70 characters  
* **Keyword placement:** Primary keyword included naturally  
* **Format:** Clear, benefit-driven, question or statement  
* **Avoid:** Generic titles, keyword stuffing, clickbait without substance  
* **Good:** `Answer Engine Optimization: Get Cited by ChatGPT in 30 Days`  
* **Bad:** `Everything You Need to Know About AEO` (too generic)

## **Introduction Structure (First 300 Words)**

### **Opening Hook (First 2-3 Sentences)**

* **Purpose:** Grab attention with data or surprising fact  
* **Format:** `[Shocking statistic] + [Why it matters] + [What you'll learn]`  
* **Length:** 2-3 sentences, 40-60 words total  
* **Include:** Numbers, specific outcomes, urgency  
* **Example:**

```
ChatGPT reaches 58 million monthly searches in the US alone. That's more search volume than 99% of keywords in existence. Your brand either gets cited in AI responses or becomes invisible.
```

### **Problem Statement (Next 2-3 Sentences)**

* **Purpose:** Articulate the pain point  
* **Format:** `[Current state] + [Problem] + [Consequence]`  
* **Length:** 2-3 sentences, 40-60 words  
* **Tone:** Empathetic but direct  
* **Example:**

```
Traditional SEO fights for ten spots on page one. AEO fights for citations in a single AI-generated answer. Most businesses haven't adapted, giving early movers a 3.4x traffic advantage.
```

### **Solution Preview (Next 2-3 Sentences)**

* **Purpose:** Introduce your solution/framework  
* **Format:** `[What you'll learn] + [Proof point] + [Outcome promise]`  
* **Length:** 2-3 sentences, 40-60 words  
* **Include:** Specific methodology, data, results  
* **Example:**

```
This guide shows the exact framework we used to grow Qcall.ai to 93 ChatGPT citations in 90 days. Every tactic is backed by Ahrefs data and real case studies. You'll get the complete 30-day implementation roadmap.
```

### **Table of Contents (For Articles 2,000+ Words)**

* **Placement:** After introduction, before first H2  
* **Format:** Numbered or bulleted list with anchor links  
* **Purpose:** Help AI systems understand content structure  
* **Include:** All H2 headings (not H3)  
* **Clickable:** Add anchor links for user navigation  
* **Example:**

```
## Table of Contents
1. What is AEO (And Why It Matters Now)
2. How LLMs Actually Choose Citations
3. AEO vs SEO: What Changed
4. Technical Foundation For AEO
5. Content Strategy For Maximum Citations
```

## **Body Content Structure**

### **H2 Heading Structure (Main Sections)**

* **Format:** Question-based whenever possible  
* **Length:** 40-60 characters  
* **Frequency:** Every 300-500 words  
* **Keyword inclusion:** Include LSI keywords naturally  
* **Pattern:** Use "How," "What," "Why," "When" questions  
* **Good:** `How Do LLMs Actually Choose Citations?`  
* **Bad:** `LLM Citation Process` (not conversational)

### **H3 Heading Structure (Subsections)**

* **Format:** More specific questions or statement subtopics  
* **Length:** 30-50 characters  
* **Frequency:** Every 150-250 words within H2 sections  
* **Relationship:** Must relate directly to parent H2  
* **Pattern:** Drill down into specifics  
* \*\*Example under "How Do LLMs Choose Citations?":  
  * `What Is The Citation Selection Process?`  
  * `Why Does Content Depth Matter?`  
  * `How Do Authority Signals Impact Citations?`

### **Answer Capsule Structure (Critical Component)**

* **Placement:** Immediately after EVERY H2 and H3 heading  
* **Length:** 1-3 sentences (20-60 words)  
* **Format:** Direct answer, no preamble  
* **Purpose:** Give AI systems quotable, extractable content  
* **Structure:** `[Direct answer with data] + [Why it matters] + [Supporting detail]`  
* **Links:** Minimal to none in the capsule itself (place below)  
* \*\*Example after "How Long Does AEO Take To Work?":

```
Based on our case studies, you can see significant citation increases within 90 days of consistent effort. Featured snippets and FAQ answers can appear within 2-4 weeks. Citations in ChatGPT and Perplexity typically take 1-2 months.
```

### **Supporting Paragraph Structure**

* **Length:** 2-3 sentences maximum per paragraph  
* **First sentence:** Topic sentence with key point  
* **Second sentence:** Supporting detail or data  
* **Third sentence:** Implication or transition (optional)  
* **White space:** Leave blank line between paragraphs  
* **Example:**

```
Content featuring original statistics sees 30-40% higher visibility in LLM responses. Pages with 19+ statistical data points averaged 5.4 citations compared to 2.8 for pages with minimal data.

This means you need to prioritize original research. Customer surveys, platform data analysis, and industry benchmarks all qualify as citable original data.
```

### **Section Length Optimization**

* **Optimal range:** 120-180 words between headings  
* **Minimum:** Never less than 50 words  
* **Maximum:** Break up sections over 250 words with H3  
* **Why:** Research shows 120-180 word sections average 4.6 citations vs 2.7 for \<50 word sections

## **Data Presentation Structure**

### **Comparison Table Structure**

* **Placement:** Within relevant sections, not random  
* **Header row:** Clear, descriptive column titles  
* **Left column:** Item/category being compared  
* **Format:** Use ONLY ✓ and ✗ emoji for yes/no  
* **No:** Other emoji, checkmarks, X's, colors in text  
* **Alignment:** Left-align text, center-align emoji  
* **Caption:** Include table title above  
* **Example:**

```
| Content Type | Citation Share | Recommended |
|--------------|---------------|-------------|
| Listicles | 32% | ✓ |
| Blog posts | 9.9% | ✓ |
| How-to guides | 8.7% | ✓ |
| Thin content (<800 words) | 3.2% | ✗ |
```

### **Statistical Data Presentation**

* **Format:** `[Specific number] + [context] + [source/timeframe]`  
* **Bold:** Numbers for visual scanning  
* **Include:** Unit of measurement, timeframe, comparison  
* **Good:** `Pages with 19+ data points averaged 5.4 citations vs 2.8 for minimal data`  
* **Bad:** `More data gets more citations` (too vague)

### **List Structure (Bullets and Numbers)**

* **Bullets:** For unordered items of equal importance  
* **Numbers:** For sequential steps or ranked items  
* **Length:** 5-10 items optimal (not more than 15\)  
* **Format per item:** 1-2 sentences maximum  
* **Intro:** Lead with sentence introducing the list  
* **Parallel structure:** Start each item same way (verb, noun, etc.)  
* **Example:**

```
What actually works for AEO:
- Content depth and comprehensiveness (strongest signal)
- Expert quotes (4.1 citations vs 2.4 without)
- Statistical data points (19+ data points = 5.4 citations vs 2.8)
- Consistent heading structure
- Readability (Flesch Score 90+ optimal)
```

### **Quote Structure (Expert Insights)**

* **Format:** Blockquote or quotation marks  
* **Attribution:** Name, title, company  
* **Placement:** After relevant claim or data point  
* **Length:** 1-3 sentences  
* **Purpose:** Build E-E-A-T authority  
* **Example:**

```
"The single biggest factor in AEO success is answer-first content structure," says John Smith, Head of SEO at TechCorp. "Sites that implement answer capsules see 70% more citations than those that bury answers in paragraphs."
```

## **FAQ Section Structure**

### **Section Placement**

* **Position:** After main content, before conclusion  
* **H2 heading:** "Frequently Asked Questions" or topic-specific  
* **Separator:** Visual break from main content (line or spacing)

### **Individual FAQ Structure**

* **Question format (H3):** Natural language question users actually ask  
* **Length:** 5-15 words  
* **Start with:** Who, What, When, Where, Why, How, Is, Can, Do, Does  
* **Example:** `How Long Does AEO Take To Work?`

### **Answer Structure Per FAQ**

* **First sentence:** Direct answer (answer capsule principle)  
* **Length:** 50-150 words total  
* **Format:** 2-4 sentences with supporting detail  
* **Include:** Data, examples, or specific timeframes  
* **Avoid:** Vague generalities, "it depends" without explanation  
* **Example:**

```
### How Long Does AEO Take To Work?

Based on our case studies, you can see significant citation increases within 90 days of consistent effort. Featured snippets and FAQ answers can appear within 2-4 weeks. Citations in ChatGPT and Perplexity typically take 1-2 months. Qcall.ai hit 93 ChatGPT citations in 90 days. SEOengine.ai reached 20 citations in 30 days.
```

### **FAQ Section Length**

* **Minimum:** 10 questions for standard articles  
* **Optimal:** 15-20 questions for comprehensive guides  
* **Maximum:** 25 questions (beyond this, split into categories)  
* **Keyword optimization:** Each question should target LSI keyword

### **FAQ Schema Preparation**

* **Structure:** Match schema.org FAQPage format  
* **Clean text:** Answers should work as plain text  
* **No HTML:** Avoid complex formatting in FAQ answers  
* **Complete:** Each answer must be self-contained

## **Visual Content Structure**

### **Image Structure**

* **Placement:** After relevant text section, not before  
* **Frequency:** Every 300-500 words minimum  
* **Alt text:** Descriptive, include keywords naturally (10-15 words)  
* **Caption:** Optional, but helpful for context  
* **File name:** Descriptive with keywords (use hyphens)  
* **Example alt text:** `Ahrefs keyword data showing answer engine optimization search volume`

### **Screenshot Structure**

* **Purpose:** Support claims with visual proof  
* **Placement:** Immediately after mentioning the data  
* **Caption:** Explain what the screenshot shows  
* **Alt text:** Describe the data visible in image  
* **Quality:** High resolution, readable text

### **Chart/Graph Structure**

* **Type:** Bar charts, line graphs, pie charts  
* **Title:** Clear description above chart  
* **Labels:** All axes and data points labeled  
* **Source:** Attribution if using external data  
* **Alt text:** Describe the trend or main finding

### **Infographic Structure**

* **Length:** Vertical format, not too long  
* **Text:** Minimal, readable at small sizes  
* **Data:** Focus on 5-7 key points  
* **Branding:** Include logo but not prominently  
* **Alt text:** Summary of main points covered

---

## **Internal Linking Structure**

### **Anchor Text Format**

* **Natural:** Part of sentence flow  
* **Descriptive:** Indicate what user will find  
* **Length:** 2-5 words typically  
* **Keyword-rich:** Include target keyword naturally  
* **Avoid:** "Click here," "Learn more," "Read this"  
* **Good:** `Learn about schema markup implementation`  
* **Bad:** `Click here for schema info`

### **Link Placement**

* **Position:** Within supporting paragraphs, not answer capsules  
* **Frequency:** 2-5 internal links per 1,000 words  
* **Relevance:** Only link to directly related content  
* **Depth:** Link to deep pages, not just homepage  
* **Context:** Provide context before and after link

### **Link Priority**

* **Primary links:** Pillar pages and cluster content  
* **Secondary links:** Related topics and next steps  
* **Tertiary links:** Author pages, about pages  
* **Avoid:** Over-linking, link stuffing

---

## **External Linking Structure**

### **Source Citation Links**

* **Placement:** After statistics, claims, or data points  
* **Anchor text:** Source name or "according to \[source\]"  
* **Format:** `According to [Source Name], [data point].`  
* **Opens in:** New tab (target="\_blank")  
* **Authority:** Link only to authoritative sources

### **Reference Link Format**

* **Position:** Natural within sentence  
* **Purpose:** Support claims, provide context  
* **Anchor:** Specific page title or source name  
* **Frequency:** 3-8 external links per article  
* **Quality:** High domain authority sites only

### **External Link Quality Checklist**

* **Authority:** Domain Rating 50+ preferred  
* **Relevance:** Directly related to your topic  
* **Freshness:** Recent content preferred  
* **Reputation:** Recognized, trusted sources  
* **Working:** Verify links not broken

## **Conclusion Structure**

### **Section Header**

* **H2:** "Conclusion" or action-oriented title  
* **Alternative:** `The Path Forward` or `Your Next Steps`  
* **Placement:** After FAQ section

### **Conclusion Format (4 Components)**

#### **1\. Summary of Key Takeaways (2-3 Sentences)**

* **Purpose:** Reinforce main points  
* **Format:** Bullet points or numbered list  
* **Length:** 3-5 key points  
* **Example:**

```
Here's what you need to know about AEO:
- ChatGPT reaches 58M monthly searches (larger than most keywords)
- Citations convert at 4.8% vs 0.9% for organic search
- 90-day implementation delivers measurable results
```

#### **2\. Action Timeline (2-3 Sentences)**

* **Purpose:** Set expectations  
* **Format:** Month-by-month or phase-by-phase  
* **Include:** Specific milestones  
* **Example:**

```
Month 1: Technical foundation and first citations (5-10). Month 2: Content velocity increases, citations grow to 20-30. Month 3: Authority established, citations reach 40-60+.
```

#### **3\. Success Framework Reminder (1-2 Sentences)**

* **Purpose:** Reinforce methodology  
* **Format:** Numbered steps or process  
* **Example:**

```
The framework is straightforward: build technical foundation, create answer-first content, establish cross-platform authority, measure everything, iterate based on results.
```

#### **4\. Call to Action (2-3 Sentences)**

* **Purpose:** Drive next action  
* **Format:** Direct instruction \+ benefit \+ urgency  
* **Include:** Specific action, reason why, time element  
* **Example:**

```
Start today. Implement the technical foundation. Create answer-first content. And if you need to scale content production while maintaining AEO quality, SEOengine.ai generates publication-ready articles at $5 each with no monthly commitment.
```

## **Content Length Guidelines**

### **Article Type and Target Length**

#### **Blog Post (General)**

* **Minimum:** 1,500 words  
* **Optimal:** 2,000-2,500 words  
* **Structure:** Intro (300) \+ Body (1,400-1,900) \+ FAQ (300) \+ Conclusion (200)

#### **Pillar Content**

* **Minimum:** 3,000 words  
* **Optimal:** 4,000-6,000 words  
* **Structure:** Intro (400) \+ Body (2,800-4,800) \+ FAQ (500) \+ Conclusion (300)

#### **How-To Guide**

* **Minimum:** 2,000 words  
* **Optimal:** 2,500-3,500 words  
* **Structure:** Intro (300) \+ Steps (1,800-2,800) \+ FAQ (300) \+ Conclusion (200)

#### **Comparison Article**

* **Minimum:** 2,500 words  
* **Optimal:** 3,000-4,000 words  
* **Structure:** Intro (400) \+ Comparisons (2,200-3,200) \+ FAQ (300) \+ Conclusion (200)

#### **Listicle**

* **Minimum:** 2,000 words  
* **Optimal:** 2,500-3,500 words  
* **Formula:** 200-300 words per list item  
* **Structure:** Intro (300) \+ Items (1,800-2,800) \+ FAQ (300) \+ Conclusion (200)

## **Readability Structure**

### **Sentence Structure**

* **Average length:** 15-20 words per sentence  
* **Maximum length:** Never exceed 25 words  
* **Variety:** Mix short (5-10 words) and medium (15-20 words)  
* **Avoid:** Complex compound sentences, multiple clauses  
* **Active voice:** 80%+ of sentences  
* **Passive voice:** Only when necessary

### **Paragraph Structure**

* **Average length:** 2-3 sentences (40-60 words)  
* **Maximum length:** Never exceed 4 sentences (80 words)  
* **One idea:** Each paragraph covers one point  
* **Topic sentence:** First sentence states main point  
* **White space:** Blank line between all paragraphs

### **Word Choice**

* **Reading level:** 8th grade or lower  
* **Syllables:** Prefer 1-2 syllable words  
* **Jargon:** Explain technical terms on first use  
* **Clarity:** Specific words over general terms  
* **Flesch Score:** Target 90+ for maximum accessibility

### **Transition Words**

* **Between paragraphs:** Use transition words (However, Additionally, Therefore)  
* **Between sections:** Use transition sentences  
* **Flow:** Ensure logical progression of ideas  
* **Coherence:** Each sentence relates to previous

## **Content Formatting Rules**

### **Bold Text Usage**

* **Purpose:** Emphasize key points, numbers, important terms  
* **Frequency:** 1-2 bold phrases per 100 words  
* **Apply to:** Statistics, key findings, important warnings  
* **Avoid:** Overuse, entire sentences, promotional content

### **Italic Text Usage**

* **Purpose:** Slight emphasis, introduce new terms, quotes  
* **Frequency:** Sparingly, less than bold  
* **Apply to:** New terminology, foreign words, book/product titles  
* **Avoid:** Long passages, statistics, key findings (use bold instead)

### **Code Blocks**

* **Purpose:** Show technical examples, code snippets  
* **Format:** Use triple backticks with language identifier  
* **Syntax:** Include syntax highlighting when possible  
* **Example:**

```json
{
  "keyword": "answer engine optimization",
  "volume": 2500
}
```

### **Blockquotes**

* **Purpose:** Highlight important quotes, key takeaways  
* **Format:** Use \> or blockquote HTML tag  
* **Length:** 1-3 sentences maximum  
* **Attribution:** Include source after quote

### **Horizontal Rules**

* **Purpose:** Separate major sections visually  
* **Frequency:** Between major topic changes  
* **Format:** `---` in Markdown or `<hr>` in HTML  
* **Avoid:** Overuse, between every section

## **Mobile Optimization Structure**

### **Responsive Considerations**

* **Paragraph length:** Even shorter on mobile (2 sentences max)  
* **List length:** Fewer items visible, keep to 5-7  
* **Table width:** Ensure horizontal scrolling works  
* **Image size:** Optimize for mobile loading  
* **Font size:** Minimum 16px for body text

### **Touch Targets**

* **Links:** Minimum 44x44 pixels  
* **Buttons:** Large, easy to tap  
* **Spacing:** Adequate space between clickable elements

---

## **Content Update Structure**

### **Update Indicators**

* **Last updated date:** Display prominently at top  
* **Update note:** Brief explanation of what changed  
* **Changelog:** For major updates, list changes  
* **Version:** Optional for technical content

### **Freshness Signals**

* **Current year in title:** Update annually  
* **Recent statistics:** Update quarterly  
* **Expert quotes:** Refresh annually  
* **Case studies:** Add new examples yearly  
* **Screenshots:** Update when UI changes

## **Final Content Checklist Structure**

### **Pre-Publish Verification**

1. **Meta data:** Title (50-53 chars), description (140 chars) ✓  
2. **H1:** One per page, keyword included ✓  
3. **TL;DR:** Present and compelling ✓  
4. **Headings:** Question-based H2/H3 ✓  
5. **Answer capsules:** After every heading ✓  
6. **Paragraphs:** 2-3 sentences each ✓  
7. **Word count:** 2,000+ words for standard, 4,000+ for pillar ✓  
8. **FAQ section:** 10-20 questions ✓  
9. **Conclusion:** Includes CTA ✓  
10. **Schema:** Article and FAQ schema implemented ✓  
11. **Images:** Alt text on all images ✓  
12. **Links:** Internal and external links working ✓  
13. **Readability:** Flesch score 90+ ✓  
14. **Keywords:** Primary keyword density 1.5%+, LSI 3%+ ✓  
15. **Tables:** Comparison tables with ✓/✗ only ✓
