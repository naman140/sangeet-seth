## What Is The Step-By-Step Automation Architecture?

The step-by-step automation architecture begins with a full operational audit, maps out all manual data flows, selects the proper integration platforms, and deploys sequences logically. It is strictly sequential and avoids overwhelming the user.

Most coaches fail at automation because they try to connect random Zapier templates without a master plan. You must architect the foundation first.

Follow this exact deployment cycle:
1. Map your current client journey on a whiteboard.
2. Identify the single most painful administrative bottleneck.
3. Build the automation for that specific bottleneck in Make.com.
4. Test with dummy data, then push to live.

This methodical approach guarantees that nothing breaks during the transition.

### Should I Use Zapier or Make.com?

You should use Make.com for complex, multi-step coaching automations because its visual router logic is far superior to Zapier. Make.com handles conditional branching—like sending different emails based on a payment plan—effortlessly.

Zapier is excellent for simple, linear tasks, but professional operations command the resilience and cost-efficiency of Make.com.

### How Hard Is It To Maintain Automations?

Automations are incredibly simple to maintain once properly built. They run silently in the background, only alerting you if an external tool changes its API or requires re-authentication.

We structure error-handling modules that catch potential failures before they impact a client, sending a private Slack notification directly to your phone.
